📖 Project Overview
Stats Central is a centralized sports betting intelligence platform that combines real-time stats, standings, player performance, and betting odds in one mobile app.  

This project serves as a comprehensive case study in digital product development, covering market research, user persona creation, competitive analysis, and monetization strategy.
🔍 The Problem Space
Through our market research, we identified a fractured user experience in the sports betting ecosystem.
Bettors bounce between ESPN, Twitter/X, Reddit & betting apps just to make one informed wager.  

Gathering reliable data is slow, inconsistent & often overwhelming before tip-off or kickoff.  

Incomplete info leads to bad bets and real financial losses for casual and serious bettors alike.  

✨ Proposed Solution & Core Features
Right now, those four things - live stats, standings, injuries, and betting odds - live in four different apps. We pulled them onto one screen. The proposed platform includes:  

Live Stats & Scores: Real-time game data across NBA, NFL, MLB & EPL.  

Injury Reports: Up-to-date player availability so you know who's in or out.  

Standings & Rankings: Live conference & division standings updated continuously.  

Betting Odds: Spreads, moneylines & totals for every upcoming matchup.  

👥 User Personas & Journey
Our UX research focused on user archetypes within the 50M+ active sports bettors in the US:  

Rick (22, entry-level finance in Manhattan, NYC): A tech-comfortable user who wants to research a game in 10-20 minutes and make data-backed bets, not gut bets. His main frustration is that stats are stale by the time he reads them, and he wants to avoid bouncing between 5 apps.  

Mike (47, IT Manager in Jersey City, NJ): A Sunday NFL parlay builder who relies heavily on scanning injury reports before the early games. His main goal is to build a Sunday parlay with confidence and spot value lines before they move.  

💼 Conceptual Business Model
As part of the product strategy, we modeled a freemium monetization funnel:  

Free Tier ($0/mo): Ad-supported access to basic stats & scores, top-line standings, and limited daily odds.  

Pro Tier ($7.99/mo): Ad-free experience with all live stats & injury reports, full betting odds + line movement, and a multi-sport dashboard.  

Affiliate Partnerships: Co-market with DraftKings & FanDuel for user acquisition.  

🏗️ Execution & Technical Roadmap
Our group mapped out a product rollout using a phased approach:
Completed for Academic Project
Phase 1 (MVP Build): Core stats, scores & basic odds. We built it in React and wrapped it as a native iOS app. Right now it runs on mock data, which let us validate the entire user experience without spending a dollar on a sports data API.  

Hypothetical Future Phases (If scaled to production)
Phase 2 (Beta Launch): A closed beta with a small group of bettors to validate the freemium funnel and collect user feedback.  

Phase 3 (App Store Launch): Public iOS release, Pro tier live. This phase involves plugging in a real-time data feed, with SportRadar or the Odds API as the leading candidates.  

Phase 4 (Growth): Marketing push and growth through partnerships with the betting platforms themselves.
